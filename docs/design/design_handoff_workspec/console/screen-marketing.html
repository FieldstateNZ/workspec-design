A NOTE FROM THE FOUNDERS

We built the tool we kept not finding.

We've shipped product at companies you've heard of. Every team had the same disease: a graveyard of stale Notion docs, a Slack channel that became the source of truth by accident, and a PM and an engineer who couldn't agree on what "auth" meant.

The tools we tried optimized for the artifacts (docs, tickets, diagrams) and ignored the connective tissue between them. So context drifted, decisions got re-litigated, and the people closest to the work spent half their time on archaeology.

Workspec is what we wanted: a graph that holds the connective tissue, an agent that keeps it true, and a UI that gets out of the way. We hope it gives you back the half of your week you've been losing to translation.

FAQ

The short answers.

Didn't find your question? Email us at hello@workspec.io.

How is this different from Notion or Linear? +
Notion is a doc tool — text in containers. Linear is a ticket tool — work in queues. Workspec is the graph between them: the typed nodes (notes, features, scenarios, services) and the typed edges (sources, feeds, blocks). It's the connective tissue both of those tools assume someone else is keeping.
Does Atlas auto-edit my work? +
Never without showing you. Atlas drafts as ghost artifacts (dashed outlines), and shows every tool call in its thinking trace. You approve or reject. You can disable autonomous actions entirely in settings.
Where does my data live? +
US-East by default, EU-West on request. Team plan and above include audit logging. Enterprise customers can self-host or run in their own VPC. We're SOC 2 Type II certified.
Which models does Atlas use? +
Claude Sonnet 4 by default, with task-specific routing. Enterprise customers can pin specific models or bring their own keys (Anthropic, OpenAI, or self-hosted).
Can I export everything? +
Yes. Export to JSON, Markdown, or a static HTML site. Your graph is yours — we don't lock it in.
